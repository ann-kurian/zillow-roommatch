Product Overview

What Zillow does — and why it matters

Zillow is the United States' leading integrated housing marketplace — allowing users to buy, sell, and rent homes, with access to property listings, price estimates, neighbourhood data, and market trends. For renters specifically, the platform's rental search feature offers map-based browsing, detailed filters, virtual tours, and comprehensive listing information that makes it the dominant starting point for apartment hunting in the US.

The rental search feature is built around a core user journey: search by location, browse results in list or map view, apply filters for price and preferences, and click through to detailed listings. For most renters, this workflow is smooth and well-executed. But for a specific and growing segment — those looking to share a rental with a roommate — Zillow's current product has a meaningful gap.

Problem Statement
"Zillow does not provide a one-stop solution for users seeking rentals with an intention to share with roommates — forcing them to manage two separate searches across two different platforms."
Existing Feature Analysis

How the rental search currently works

Mapping the existing user flow reveals a well-designed core experience — but one that terminates at the listing level. Once a user finds a property they're interested in sharing, there is no pathway to find or connect with potential roommates without leaving the platform entirely.

01
Sign In
User authenticates via multiple login options — Google, Apple, email. Simple and familiar onboarding.
02
Location Search
User searches by area or neighbourhood of interest. Autocomplete and map integration make this fast and intuitive.

Relevant listings returned in a scrollable list with key details — price, bedrooms, distance, photos.

Results — Map View

User refines results by price range, bedroom count, pet policy, and other preferences. Well-designed but doesn't include a co-tenant or roommate filter. ← The gap begins here.
SWOT Analysis

Where Zillow is strong — and where it isn't

Strengths
Extensive, up-to-date property database with national coverage
Powerful search filters returning precise, personalised results
Detailed listings with photos, virtual tours, and feature descriptions
Interactive map-based browsing
Strong brand recognition and trust in the real estate market
Weaknesses
No roommate finder or matching feature — users must leave the platform
Recommendation engine does not account for past search behaviour
No commute-time or nearby amenity search from a specific listing
Opportunities
Launch a roommate matching feature targeting students and young professionals
Transition into an end-to-end platform: applications, digital lease signing, rent payments, maintenance requests
Threats
Competition from Apartments.com, Trulia, Craigslist, and Facebook Marketplace
Housing market instability affecting user behaviour patterns
Uncertainty in rental regulations and policies across US states
User Personas

Who the feature is built for

The roommate-finding gap matters most for two cohorts: students relocating for study and early-career professionals in new cities. Both are cost-sensitive, time-constrained, and — crucially — lack established social networks in the places they're moving to. Zillow already serves both groups for property discovery; RoomMatch closes the loop.

Avery
Budget-Conscious Student
Age 21 · Relocating for university
Needs
Affordable rent, safe living conditions, reliable company in an unfamiliar city.
Pain Points
Tight budget, limited contacts in the new city, no reliable way to vet potential roommates from a distance.
Goal
Find a trustworthy roommate to split rent and living expenses before arriving in the city.
John
Early-Career Professional
Age 26 · New to the city for work
Needs
Budget-friendly rent close to the workplace, a compatible roommate, and a smooth move-in process.
Pain Points
Limited time due to work commitments, difficulty finding like-minded roommates, rent that scales with an early-career salary.
Goal
Match with a compatible roommate quickly — ideally someone with similar work hours and lifestyle — without a lengthy search process.

Why RoomMatch?

Value for both sides

A new feature is only worth building if it creates clear value for users and a defensible strategic advantage for the product. RoomMatch does both — it solves a real, unmet user need while simultaneously improving Zillow's engagement, retention, and monetisation metrics.

For Users
Reduces effective rent through cost-sharing — directly addresses the #1 pain point of Zillow's student and early-career cohort
Eliminates the need to switch between Zillow (for listings) and Craigslist or Facebook (for roommates)
Preference-based matching reduces time spent on incompatible conversations
In-app messaging removes the friction and risk of sharing personal contact details with strangers
Particularly valuable for users relocating from out of state or internationally, who have no existing local network
For Zillow
Increases session depth and time-in-app by adding a social engagement layer to a search-only product
Creates a new monetisation pathway — premium matching tiers, background checks, verified profiles
Improves user retention by making Zillow the complete end-to-end rental platform, not just a discovery tool
Expands the addressable user base to include users who weren't previously searching for their own unit
Differentiates from Apartments.com, Trulia, and other competitors — none of whom offer integrated roommate matching
Success Metrics

How we'd know it's working

Good PM thinking always ends with measurability. These five metrics would form the core of a RoomMatch launch dashboard — covering activation, engagement, outcome quality, and retention.

📊
% of rental searches with RoomMatch enabled
Feature adoption rate — tracks how many users in the target cohort activate RoomMatch during their rental search.
🤝
Number of successful roommate matches per month
Core outcome metric — a "match" defined as mutual connection request + in-app message exchange.
⏱️
Average time to find a match
Quality of the matching algorithm — shorter time-to-match indicates better preference alignment and UX efficiency.
🔄
User retention rate among RoomMatch users
Retention lift — do users who engage with RoomMatch continue to use Zillow after finding a roommate?
📈
% increase in new users via RoomMatch
Growth metric — tracks whether RoomMatch attracts new users to Zillow who were previously using competing platforms for roommate discovery.
🎯
North Star: Listings saved + RoomMatch match in same session
The single metric that captures RoomMatch's core value — a user who finds both a property and a potential roommate without leaving Zillow.
Reflection

What this project demonstrates

This teardown started from a simple observation: Zillow is excellent at helping people find where to live, but it completely ignores the question of who to live with — even though those two decisions are often made simultaneously, especially for students and young professionals on a budget.

The RoomMatch proposal emerged directly from the SWOT analysis rather than being a pre-formed idea. Identifying the absence of a roommate finder as the most structurally addressable weakness — and cross-referencing it with the opportunity space for an integrated platform — is where the feature idea came from. The personas then grounded the proposal in specific, realistic user behaviour rather than an abstract user need.

"The best feature proposals don't solve hypothetical problems — they solve the friction that users are already working around. Zillow's users don't stop needing a roommate just because Zillow doesn't offer one. They just go to Craigslist."
Ann Mary Kurian · Product Strategy
